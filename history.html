History & Origin of Turbans

Exploring the rich heritage across civilizations and centuries

3000 BCE

Earliest depictions of turban-like headwear appear in Mesopotamian art and sculptures, showing wrapped cloth head coverings.

8th Century

Turbans became prominent in Islamic cultures, adopted by Muslim leaders and scholars across the Middle East and North Africa.

15th Century

Sikh Gurus in South Asia popularized the turban as a mandatory religious article, symbolizing spirituality and equality.

17th Century

Ottoman Empire established elaborate turban styles as markers of social status and profession.

19th Century

British colonial period saw turbans being adapted by various military regiments in South Asia.

21st Century

Modern turbans have become global fashion statements while maintaining cultural and religious significance.

Cultural Origins

South Asia

The dastaar in Sikhism, pagri in Hindu traditions, and various regional styles across India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh.

Middle East

Keffiyeh, ghutra, and other styles worn across Arab nations, often with agal (rope band) to hold them in place.

North Africa

Tagelmust worn by Tuareg people, often indigo-dyed and serving as protection from desert conditions.
